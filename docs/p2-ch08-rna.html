8  RNA & Transcript-Level Models

Warning

Main decision point: keep this focused on RNA as a molecule (structure, sequence models, ncRNA) vs. the existing DNA→RNA prediction content in Ch 7/9. The cleanest split treats splicing as a DNA-sequence prediction problem (Ch 7) and RNA-seq/expression as a hybrid model output (Ch 9), while this new chapter covers models that operate on RNA sequences directly.

8.1 Proposed Position

Part II, Chapter 8 (after Splicing, before Tokens chapter)

8.2 Chapter Outline

8.2.1 8.1 Introduction: Why RNA Modeling Is Different

  • RNA as molecule vs. transcript annotation
  • Secondary structure as a first-class constraint
  • The gap between DNA-centric models and RNA-specific biology

8.2.2 8.2 RNA Secondary Structure Prediction

  • Classical approaches (thermodynamic folding, comparative)
  • Deep learning methods (SPOT-RNA, etc.)
  • Structure-aware attention mechanisms

8.2.3 8.3 RNA Foundation Models

  • RNA-FM: self-supervised pretraining on 23M ncRNA sequences
  • RNAErnie, RNA-MSM, and related models
  • Downstream tasks: structure prediction, protein-RNA binding, expression regulation

8.2.4 8.4 Codon-Level Models

  • cdsFM (EnCodon/DeCodon): synonymous codon context
  • Life-Code: central dogma unification with codon tokenization
  • Why protein LMs miss codon usage signals

8.2.5 8.5 mRNA Design & Optimization

  • Codon optimization for expression
  • UTR design for stability and translation efficiency
  • Applications: vaccine design, mRNA therapeutics

8.2.6 8.6 Non-coding RNA Classification & Function

  • lncRNA, miRNA, circRNA classification
  • Target prediction for regulatory RNAs

8.2.7 8.7 Clinical Applications

  • Splice-altering variant interpretation (cross-ref to Ch 7)
  • RNA-based therapeutics target identification
