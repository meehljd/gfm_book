16.1 Chapter Overview

  • Motivation: Why a survey chapter distinct from evaluation methodology?
  • Relationship to Chapter 17: this chapter catalogs what exists; Ch17 covers how to evaluate properly
  • A rapidly evolving landscape: benchmarks as snapshots of community priorities

16.2 Molecular and Regulatory Benchmarks

16.2.1 Chromatin and TF Binding Tasks

  • DeepSEA evaluation sets: ENCODE/Roadmap chromatin profiles
  • Basset and Basenji evaluation paradigms
  • Nucleotide Transformer regulatory tasks
  • Limitations: cell line bias, assay heterogeneity

16.2.2 Splicing Benchmarks

  • SpliceAI validation: GTEx RNA-seq, validation rates by delta score
  • GENCODE splice junction sets
  • Clinical splice variant databases
  • Coverage gaps: deep intronic regions, tissue specificity

16.2.3 Expression Prediction Tasks

  • GTEx-based expression benchmarks
  • Enformer evaluation: cross-gene, cross-individual splits
  • Sei regulatory variant assessments
  • Open questions: cell type coverage, dynamic range

16.3 DNA Language Model Benchmark Suites

16.3.1 BEND (Benchmark for DNA Language Models)

  • Task composition: gene finding, enhancer annotation, chromatin accessibility, histone marks, CpG methylation
  • Design philosophy: biologically meaningful tasks on human genome
  • Key findings: limited long-range feature capture in current models

16.3.2 Genomics Long-Range Benchmark (LRB)

  • InstaDeep/Nucleotide Transformer evaluation suite
  • Tasks requiring extended context: enhancer-promoter linking, expression prediction
  • Fine-tuning recipes and their impact on evaluation
  • Performance gaps: variant effect and expression prediction lag behind annotation tasks

16.3.3 DNALongBench

  • Five long-range tasks up to 1 Mb: enhancer-target gene, eQTL, 3D organization, regulatory activity, transcription initiation
  • Baseline comparisons: CNN, HyenaDNA, Caduceus variants
  • Designed for standardized long-range dependency assessment

16.3.4 GenBench

  • Modular framework for GFM evaluation
  • Short-range vs long-range task stratification
  • Architecture-task interactions: attention vs convolution preferences
  • Reproducibility emphasis

16.4 Protein Language Model Benchmarks

16.4.1 ProteinGym

  • 217 deep mutational scanning assays
  • Standardized variant effect prediction evaluation
  • Coverage of diverse protein families
  • Spearman correlation as primary metric; limitations thereof

16.4.2 Structure Prediction Benchmarks

  • CASP legacy and ESMFold evaluation
  • TM-score, GDT-TS metrics
  • Single-sequence vs MSA-based evaluation

16.4.3 Clinical Variant Benchmarks for Proteins

  • ClinVar pathogenic vs benign discrimination
  • Benchmarking AlphaMissense, ESM-1v, CADD integration
  • Circularity concerns: training on clinical labels

16.5 Variant Effect Prediction Benchmarks

16.5.1 Clinical Annotation Databases

  • ClinVar: composition, submission biases, version sensitivity
  • HGMD limitations and access issues
  • Expert-curated panels (ClinGen, disease-specific)
  • Ancestry and gene coverage biases

16.5.2 Multiplexed Assays of Variant Effect (MAVEs)

  • Deep mutational scanning: experimental ground truth
  • MaveDB as a resource
  • Assay-specific biases: reporter context, selection stringency
  • Gene coverage gaps

16.5.3 Noncoding Variant Benchmarks

  • MPRA datasets for regulatory variants
  • eQTL-based evaluation approaches
  • GWAS fine-mapped variant sets
  • Open challenges: linking molecular to trait effects

16.6 Trait and Individual-Level Benchmarks

16.6.1 TraitGym

  • Complex trait prediction assessment
  • Evaluation of GFM-augmented polygenic scores
  • Ancestry stratification in evaluation

16.6.2 Polygenic Score Benchmarks

  • UK Biobank held-out evaluation
  • Cross-biobank transferability
  • Ancestry-specific performance gaps

16.7 Clinical and Decision-Level Benchmarks

16.7.1 Diagnostic Yield Studies

  • Exome/genome sequencing cohort evaluations
  • Retrospective re-analysis with newer models
  • Prospective validation challenges

16.7.2 Pharmacogenomic Benchmarks

  • CPIC variant prediction tasks
  • Drug response prediction evaluation

16.8 Cross-Cutting Issues

16.8.1 Benchmark Suite Proliferation

  • Fragmentation and inconsistent evaluation
  • Calls for unified evaluation frameworks
  • Tension between standardization and task-specific validity

16.8.2 What Benchmarks Miss

  • Tasks underrepresented: structural variants, repeat regions, multi-variant effects
  • Populations underrepresented: non-European ancestry
  • Modalities underrepresented: long-read data, single-cell contexts
  • Clinical endpoints vs molecular surrogates

16.8.3 Benchmark Leakage and Staleness

  • Training-test overlap across community benchmarks
  • Temporal drift: benchmarks as moving targets
  • Need for held-out evaluation consortia

16.9 Chapter Summary

  • Current benchmark landscape: strengths and systematic gaps
  • Benchmark choice shapes model development incentives
