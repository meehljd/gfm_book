# Essential Papers for Genomic Foundation Model Benchmarks Textbook Chapter

This reference compilation provides citation information for foundational, milestone, and recent papers across all major categories of genomic benchmarks relevant to foundation model evaluation.

## Protein structure prediction benchmarks

### CASP foundational paper (1994 experiment)

The Critical Assessment of protein Structure Prediction experiment began in 1994 and established the gold standard for blind evaluation in structural biology.

**Moult J, Pedersen JT, Judson R, Fidelis K.** A large-scale experiment to assess protein structure prediction methods. *Proteins: Structure, Function, and Bioinformatics* 1995; 23(3): ii-v. DOI: 10.1002/prot.340230303

### CASP14 and AlphaFold2 breakthrough (2020-2021)

CASP14 marked a transformative moment when AlphaFold2 achieved near-experimental accuracy, effectively solving a **50-year grand challenge** in biology.

**Kryshtafovych A, Schwede T, Topf M, Fidelis K, Moult J.** Critical assessment of methods of protein structure prediction (CASP)—Round XIV. *Proteins: Structure, Function, and Bioinformatics* 2021; 89(12): 1607-1617. DOI: 10.1002/prot.26237

**Jumper J, Evans R, Pritzel A, Green T, Figurnov M, Ronneberger O, et al.** Highly accurate protein structure prediction with AlphaFold. *Nature* 2021; 596(7873): 583-589. DOI: 10.1038/s41586-021-03819-2

**Tunyasuvunakool K, Adler J, Wu Z, Green T, Zielinski M, et al.** Highly accurate protein structure prediction for the human proteome. *Nature* 2021; 596(7873): 590-596. DOI: 10.1038/s41586-021-03828-1

### Protein language model benchmarks (TAPE, FLIP, ProteinGym)

**Rao R, Bhattacharya N, Thomas N, Duan Y, Chen X, Canny J, Abbeel P, Song YS.** Evaluating Protein Transfer Learning with TAPE. *Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems 32 (NeurIPS)* 2019. arXiv: 1906.08230

**Notin P, Kollasch A, Ritter D, van Niekerk L, Paul S, Spinner H, et al.** ProteinGym: Large-Scale Benchmarks for Protein Fitness Prediction and Design. *Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems 36 (NeurIPS)* 2023; 64331-64379. DOI: 10.1101/2023.12.07.570727

## Variant effect prediction benchmarks

### ClinVar database papers

ClinVar has grown from initial release to over **3 million variant submissions** from more than 2,800 organizations, making it the central public archive for genotype-phenotype relationships.

**Landrum MJ, Lee JM, Riley GR, Jang W, Rubinstein WS, Church DM, Maglott DR.** ClinVar: public archive of relationships among sequence variation and human phenotype. *Nucleic Acids Research* 2014; 42(D1): D980-D985. DOI: 10.1093/nar/gkt1113

**Landrum MJ, Kattman BL.** ClinVar at Five Years: Delivering on the Promise. *Human Mutation* 2018; 39(11): 1623-1630. DOI: 10.1002/humu.23641

**Landrum MJ, Chitipiralla S, Kaur K, Brown G, Chen C, et al.** ClinVar: updates to support classifications of both germline and somatic variants. *Nucleic Acids Research* 2025; 53(D1): D1313-D1321. DOI: 10.1093/nar/gkae1090

### ClinVar submission heterogeneity

**Yang S, Lincoln SE, Kobayashi Y, Nykamp K, Nussbaum RL, Topper S.** Sources of discordance among germ-line variant classifications in ClinVar. *Genetics in Medicine* 2017; 19(10): 1118-1126. DOI: 10.1038/gim.2017.60

**Harrison SM, Dolinsky JS, Knight Johnson AE, Pesaran T, Azzariti DR, et al.** Clinical laboratories collaborate to resolve differences in variant interpretations submitted to ClinVar. *Genetics in Medicine* 2017; 19(10): 1096-1104. DOI: 10.1038/gim.2017.14

### CAGI (Critical Assessment of Genome Interpretation)

**Critical Assessment of Genome Interpretation Consortium.** CAGI, the Critical Assessment of Genome Interpretation, establishes progress and prospects for computational genetic variant interpretation methods. *Genome Biology* 2024; 25(1): 53. DOI: 10.1186/s13059-023-03113-6

**Andreoletti G, Pal LR, Moult J, Brenner SE.** Reports from the fifth edition of CAGI: The Critical Assessment of Genome Interpretation. *Human Mutation* 2019; 40(9): 1197-1201. DOI: 10.1002/humu.23876

### MaveDB (deep mutational scanning data)

MaveDB aggregates data from multiplexed assays of variant effect, now containing over **7 million variant effects** across hundreds of proteins.

**Esposito D, Weile J, Shendure J, Starita LM, Papenfuss AT, Roth FP, Fowler DM, Rubin AF.** MaveDB: an open-source platform to distribute and interpret data from multiplexed assays of variant effect. *Genome Biology* 2019; 20(1): 223. DOI: 10.1186/s13059-019-1845-6

**Rubin AF, Stone J, Bianchi AH, Capodanno BJ, Da EY, Dias M, et al.** MaveDB 2024: a curated community database with over seven million variant effects from multiplexed functional assays. *Genome Biology* 2025; 26(1): 13. DOI: 10.1186/s13059-025-03476-y

### Deep mutational scanning benchmarking

**Livesey BJ, Marsh JA.** Using deep mutational scanning to benchmark variant effect predictors and identify disease mutations. *Molecular Systems Biology* 2020; 16(7): e9380. DOI: 10.15252/msb.20199380

**Livesey BJ, Marsh JA.** Updated benchmarking of variant effect predictors using deep mutational scanning. *Molecular Systems Biology* 2023; 19(8): e11474. DOI: 10.15252/msb.202211474

## Benchmark methodology papers

### Data leakage in genomics

Sequence similarity and homology-based leakage remain **underappreciated confounders** in genomic ML benchmarks, leading to systematically inflated performance estimates.

**Bernett J, et al.** Guiding questions to avoid data leakage in biological machine learning applications. *Nature Methods* 2024; 21(8): 1444-1453. DOI: 10.1038/s41592-024-02362-y
